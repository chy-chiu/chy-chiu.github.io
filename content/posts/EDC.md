I just came back from EDC, and I want to write something about rave culture. # What is it anyway? 

The way I would describe it, it's more like Disneyland for adults. Like, it has park rides, performances, shops, music, pretty much everything you want / need, plenty of stimulation.
However, recently I saw some reel calling it a "Capitalism Shaman Retreat", and I think it was strangely accurate to an extent? 

Unlike other multi-day festivals such as Tomorrowland, EDC uniquely happens overnight from sundown to sunrise. The practical reason is that the Las Vegas desert is way too hot to party during the day. However, as someone who never wakes up early enough for sunrises, it was most definitely a vibe to party till sunrise for me.

## Rave culture, PLUR, and other Emergent cultural phenomena

Another interesting thing I observed is how American rave culture formed a solid subculture of its own. Whereas Europeans continue to wear black and basic activewear at events, it appears Americans, forever performative, dress in more colorful and diverse clothing every year. The central theme is PLUR, and along with it also came with a lot of different small an interesting traditions and rituals. The one that hung along for a while is Kandi, where people trade friendship bracelets. Naturally, with Kandi also came Pearlers and increasingly intricate engineering such as cuffs or even full pieces. 

Stragging along were also other small individual components, including trading ducks, passing on sprouts, and in the last 6-12 months ago, hot stickers. Its interesting that there si all these emergent cultural phenomena, just little mushroosm that popped up, and i think they probably started and spread like memes. In the end it is a simple transaction that do not require much energy but nonetheless it is a way for someone to pass on love and energy to another person etc.

I wonder if the random TEMU shops just one day got a million more random order for sprouts then they wondered why someone from Australia just ordered 1000 bags of ducks in particular (or in the case of kandi making for our group, small random metal spoons as a charm)
